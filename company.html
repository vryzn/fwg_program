Cast



Naomi Kim '25
Present-Day Kayleen

Naomi Kim is a freshman Theatre major at Northeastern University. Previous acting credits include Love and Information, The Theory of Relativity, The Sound of Music, and The Laramie Project. She recently graduated with four letters in drama and qualified for Thespy Excellence Awards at the International Thespian Festival. She performed last month in NYC with GPI’s costume designer, Ashley DiLorenzo, premiering Ashley’s new musical: A Stormy Night.




Jonathan Baron '24
Present-day Doug

Jonathan is a second-year student majoring in Political Science and Economics. He is from Los Angeles, but prefers the Boston snow to sunny SoCal beaches (for some reason). In his free time, he enjoys reading, creative writing, and calligraphy. On stage, he has performed stand-up comedy and improv theatre, but this is his first time acting in a play — and he’s very excited about it!




Josie Ojakli '23
Past Kayleen

Josie is a third year Theatre major with a Performance concentration and a Political Science minor. Her last credit was in the Northeastern Theatre Department’s production of “Love and Information,” and she recently spent the summer studying the Meisner Technique at the Maggie Flanigan Studio in New York. This is her first time working with New Renaissance, and she is so grateful to everyone involved for their enthusiasm and kindness, and hopes that you enjoy the show.




Sean Rowburrey '25
Past Doug

Sean is a first-year History and Political Science combined major on a Pre-Law track. Sean was featured ensemble in the NU Stage production of Jekyll and Hyde and is currently playing the roles of Mr. Brocklehurst and Colonel Dent in the NU Stage production of Jane eyre. As a member of his high school drama club for four years, Sean’s performances included Marius in Les Miserables, Harry in Mamma Mia!, Romeo in Newsies, and ensemble in All Shook Up. Sean took acting classes at a local theater and performed as a Wickersham Brother in Seussical and Perchik in Fiddler on the Roof. Sean plays the drums and other percussion instruments.





Crew



Karthik Arumugam '22
Director

Karthik is a 5th Year Mechanical Engineering Major and Theater Minor. His most recent credit is the co-director for last semester’s production of Stop Kiss. He’s was also the Assistant Electrician for NU Department of Theatre for This is Treatment. Karthik is also a member of The Beta Chi Theta Fraternity and his hobbies include Arts and Crafts, Gardening, and Engineering.




Megan Canizares '25
Assistant Director

Megan is a 1st year Communication and Media Studies major with minors in Business Administration and Theater. This is her first involvement with a production at Northeastern and she’s very proud of the cast and crew for their talent and creativity. Her hobbies include playing soccer, watching movies, and drinking lots of boba tea.




Jordan Korgood '24
Treasurer, Stage Manager

Jordan is 3rd year Marine Biology major with a minor in Environmental studies. He has performed in dozens of community theater and high school productions. This is his first production with New Renaissance. He enjoys fishing, sketch comedy, and improv in his free time.




Ashley DiLorenzo '25
Costume Designer

Ashley is a first year Media and Screen Studies and Theatre major with a songwriting minor from Queens, NY. This is her first time costume designing. Two of her short plays have been produced by Northeastern's Silver Masque over the past two semesters. Previous Northeastern productions include This is Treatment (Ms. Caroline), Love and Information (Actor 5), and The Bacchae (Light Board Operator). She's also a published playwright with YouthPLAYS and will be featured on iHeartRadio Broadway this Saturday, 4/23!




Indu Holdsworth '23
Props Director, Deck Crew

Indu is a third-year Ecology and Evolutionary Biology major with a minor in Women's Gender and Sexuality Studies. Previous credits include Props Manager for You Can't Take It With You (Bosque), Assistant Stage Manager of Urinetown (Bosque), Sound Operator for Cultural Day Shows (UWC-USA), and Stage Manager of The Box Play (UWC-USA).




Wynn Graves '24
Technical Director, Lighting Designer, Sound Designer

Wynn is a Theatre major with a Media Production minor from Moline, IL. They have previously worked on How to Survive a Pandemic (Shira Weiss and Kaitlyn Fiery) as a sound designer and board operator and As U Like It (NUTD) on set construction. They are a member of NUAGE and do work for Greenpeace outside of school.




Lou Conrad '23
General Manager, Sound Designer, Sound Board Operator

Lou is a 4th year combined major in Theatre and Media Screen Studies. Their previous Northeastern credits include Three Sisters (Ferapont), Urinetown (Mr McQueen) and working backstage in the scene shop and costume department. They also performed in their first professional show at the Apollinaire Theater in Chelsea, Hir, as Max. During the pandemic Lou participated in a few performances over zoom, but sort of disappeared from theater for a bit. They are overjoyed and grateful to be back and working with this group of amazing individuals in person. Theater is beautiful and important. Please keep others safe during this time so we can continue to share why.




Lauren McDermott '25
Makeup Artist, Deck Crew

Lauren is a 1st year Psychology major from Woodbury, New York. Recent credits include Honey in Mirror, Mirror (Acting Out), Stacee Jaxx in Rock of Ages (From Stage to Screen), and Nurse in New Ren's own production of Stop Kiss. Lauren is so glad to be a part of this crew and hopes you enjoy her ~gruesome~ work.

Makenna Harnden '23
Secretary

Makenna is a third year Media & Screen Studies in Theatre major with a minor in Theatrical Design. Makenna transferred to Northeastern University this past spring from Rhode Island College. Previous credits include Head Electrician for Love and Information (NUDoT), Assistant Electrician for As U Like It (NUDoT), Assistant Lighting Designer for Everyman (RIC) and Light Board Operator for Good Friday (RIC). Makenna is currently on co-op with Boston Playwrights' Theatre and Company One.




Mer Brown '24
Producer, Deck Crew

Mer is a 2nd year student majoring in Media and Screen Studies and Theatre and minoring in Psychology. Mer was Mrs. Winsley in Stop Kiss and Players 6, 8, and 11 in the play How To Survive a Pandemic by Kaitlyn Fiery and Shira Weiss. Mer is also a member of Silver Masque’s Technical Squadron, and recently created BitterSweet, a short film which premiered at OVV’s Horror Hang Film Festival.




Erin Rosenfeld '23
Accessibility and Advocacy Coordinator

Erin is a 3rd-year American Sign Language/Psychology combined major, an Interpreting major, and a Theatre minor. Favorite credits include Sylvia in Tribes (Glenbrook North) and Rosalind in As You Like It (Oxford University). In addition to acting, Erin is passionate about advocacy work in the Deaf community. Her efforts have led to being featured in Teen Vogue, Forbes, CBS Inside Edition, Buzzfeed, and more. She hopes you enjoy the show!




Ananya Lunkad '22
Social Media Manager

Ananya is a 4th year Communications and Media Studies major with a Theatre minor. Her most recent credit is Deck Crew for Love and Information (Theatre Department). Ananya is also a member of NUTV and her hobbies include baking, photography and film.




Hanieka Balint '22
Graphic Designer, Photographer, Captions Operator

Hanieka is a 4th year Design major, so you could say that graphic design is her passion. Her first exposure to theatre was last semester as the Graphic Designer during New Ren's Stop Kiss. When she’s not designing for class, New Ren, Scout, or her freelance clients, Hanieka can probably be found watching the X Files, reading a book in bed, or going down another Wikipedia rabbit hole.
