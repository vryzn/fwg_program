Cast



Siddie Patil '25
Shannon

Siddhie is a third year political science and business admin major who hates talking about herself because she never has any idea what to say. This is her first production with New Ren and she’s had such a blast playing Shannon over the past few months. She’s sure she has other hobbies but stress running lines for the show has kind of taken over her life at the moment. Siddhie would like to thank everyone involved in the production for making this show absolutely incredible, but also wants to shout out Chloe, Indu, Livy, and Brooke for their amazing directing, producing, and managing. This show couldn’t have been what it was without the cast and crew and she’s incredibly grateful to all the hard work everyone has put into it. She really hopes you’ll enjoy the show! Lastly she’d like to make the point that surfing in the L.A. river is entirely possible and anyone who can’t simply isn’t applying themselves enough.




Jonathan Baron '24
Josh

Jonathan is a third-year student studying Political Science and Economics, the most soul-crushing combination of subjects he could think of. Luckily for him, gingers don’t have souls, so he’s doing okay. This is Jonathan’s third production with New Renaissance Theatre Co., after having the pleasure to portray Present-Day Doug in Gruesome Playground Injuries and to direct last semester’s production of Beheld. You may also recognize him as Fortnight’s “Mac & Cheese Guy.” Jonathan would like to thank just about everyone in cast, crew, and beyond, but he’d like to give special shoutouts to Chloe for her incredible work on every last aspect of production, to Livy for being the only reason anything ever works smoothly, and to Indu for enabling his caffeine addiction like a true friend should. He’s had an absolute ball of a time with this show, and he hopes you enjoy watching it as much as he did working on it!




Violet Villanueva '23
Leah

This is Violet’s first year studying education at Northeastern. Violet is incredibly honored to be playing Leah in this production alongside such a talented cast and crew! Developing and playing this character has been such a joy, and nothing else has given her such a strong feeling of empowerment quite like this! Her previous acting work includes filmed and virtual original works of Lost Boy (Wendy), Rules of Reign (Alicina), and she most recently played Ellen in Vinegar Tom who has a similar vibe to Leah! Violet would like to thank everyone involved in this production for bringing such creativity, community, and cheerfulness into her life (extra special shout out to Chloe, Brooke, and Livy for their dedication to fantastic directing, staging, and organization!) She hopes most of all that you walk away from the show with some new perspectives!




Edward Fu '25
Danny

Edward (Eddie/Ed) has a crippling anxiety about writing his bio for this show. He enjoys drinking whiskey, eating chicken nuggets, and playing with his knife collection. He would like to thank Chloe for being the bestest peer pressuring director for making him do this show. He would also like to thank his girl-boss girlfriend Suzy for being super awesome and amazing. If you give him flowers at the end of the show, please give them to Suzy on his behalf. Don’t ask why. Yay mechanical engineering! (P.S. catch him surfing in the L.A. river this summer)





Crew



Chloe Evans '23
Director

Chloe has had the pleasure of directing this incredible cast and crew these past months, and she can’t imagine a better way to close out her time at Northeastern. Last spring she directed Henry IV, Part I with Northeastern Shakespeare Society, which ignited a passion for directing. She is so grateful to New Renaissance for giving her a chance to direct once more. She plans to graduate with a B.S. in Behavioral Neuroscience and pursue Genetic Counseling. She is so grateful to Brooke’s sweatshirt (and Brooke) for inspiring the concept for this play and for being an amazing assistant. She would also like to thank the producer, Indu, and stage managers, Livy & Ember, without them this production wouldn’t exist!




Brooke Caroom '25
Assistant Director

Brooke is a second year at Northeastern as a History major with a minor in theater. This is her first show with New Ren and first time working behind the scenes! The people and environment was so welcoming and a great experience to have all around. She is so proud of all the hard work every single member has put into this whimsical production and is thankful for all of the great memories resulting from this crazy, fun adventure! Thanks especially to Chloe for giving her this opportunity to better understand the world of theater and herself!




Livy Hanley '24
Stage Manager

Livy is a first year Northeastern student studying Biochemistry and Bioengineering and has had the privilege of working with New Ren as stage manager since the beginning of the semester. She has a history of working backstage throughout high school in productions such as Chicago and The Addams Family. The warm and caring community within New Ren has made her first production at Northeastern a wonderful experience that she hopes to continue in the future! A special thanks to Chloe and Brooke for all their talented directing and Ember and Indu for their impeccable organization and help throughout the production!
