Friends with Guns by Stephanie Alison Walker isn’t really about guns. Although the ownership and regulation of guns are central to the plot, it’s merely a lens used to understand what breaks a family. At what point is the line drawn on an issue? What happens when you’re not sure what side you belong on? What about when someone you love tries to force you to their side? This play is not black or white, it isn’t pro- or anti-gun; it’s full of color and whimsy and breathes life into two mundane families. In this perfect world of rainbows, purple benches, and puppets, where do the shadows live? When Shannon and Josh unexpectedly befriend gun-owning Leah and Danny, a monster crawls out from under the bed, exposing the flaws woven into Josh and Shannon’s marriage. Color paints every inch of this play in an attempt to suffocate the true darkness of reality and to maintain a childlike innocence where love can conquer all. How can such a gentle, vibrant world hold something so violent?
